Queensland legal forms & resources

The official Queensland tenancy links and forms in one place, plus plain-English walkthroughs for lodging a bond and dealing with unpaid rent — with a clear note on what actually applies to a homestay.

Read this first — does any of it apply to you?

A genuine homestay (living as part of a household, meals provided) is usually a boarder/lodger arrangement, which generally sits outside Queensland's Residential Tenancies and Rooming Accommodation Act. That means RTA bond lodgement and most tenancy forms below typically do NOT apply to a true homestay — and Kurrajong's model uses a refundable card hold, not a lodged bond.

They can apply if an arrangement is really a rooming accommodation (a room with shared facilities, no real "household" life) or a general tenancy. Because it's fact-specific, this page gives you both: the official RTA process if you're ever covered, and the homestay-appropriate path Kurrajong uses. This is general information, not legal advice — confirm your situation with the RTA, Queensland Fair Trading (OFT) and a lawyer.

Walkthrough: lodging & refunding a bond

This is the RTA process for a covered tenancy/rooming accommodation. For a true homestay you generally don't lodge an RTA bond — see "the homestay path" below.

How to lodge a bond if covered
  1. Collect the bond and give a receipt. Note the amount and date received — the clock starts now.
  2. Lodge it with the RTA within 10 days. A person who receives bond money must generally lodge it with the RTA within 10 days of receipt. Use RTA Web Services (fastest) or the paper Bond lodgement (Form 2).
  3. The RTA holds it for the duration — neither party keeps it. Keep your lodgement confirmation.
  4. Do an entry condition report (Form 1) at move-in so any end-of-stay claim is evidenced.
  5. At the end, refund via the Bond Refund Web Service or paper Form 4. If you claim against the bond, you must give the tenant/resident supporting evidence within 14 days (for bonds lodged on/after 30 Sept 2024).
  6. If you disagree on the refund, the RTA offers free dispute resolution; unresolved matters can go to QCAT.

Walkthrough: when rent isn't paid

The official escalation for a covered general tenancy. Rooming accommodation uses faster timeframes; a true homestay uses the path in the next section.

Unpaid rent — step by step general tenancy
  1. Talk first. A quick conversation or written reminder often fixes it — and the RTA expects you to attempt resolution.
  2. Wait until 7 clear days in arrears. You can't issue a breach notice for rent until the tenant is at least 7 days behind.
  3. Issue a Notice to remedy breach (Form 11). It must state the amount owed and give the tenant 7 clear days to pay. Send it so you can prove delivery.
  4. If they pay in time, the tenancy continues. (New rent falling due during the notice can trigger a fresh breach.)
  5. If they don't, issue a Notice to leave (Form 12) — for a rent breach the notice period is 7 clear days.
  6. If they still don't leave or pay, apply to QCAT for a termination order and warrant of possession. (Repeated breaches within 12 months can let you apply to QCAT directly.)
  7. To recover money, you can also use the RTA's free dispute resolution (Form 16); if unresolved, claim at QCAT (or its minor civil dispute process for a debt).
How rooming accommodation differs faster
  1. Shorter triggers. After 28 days' residency, a Notice to remedy breach can be issued when the resident is just 2 days in arrears, giving at least 4 days to pay.
  2. Faster exit. If unpaid, a Notice to leave can give as little as 4 days, and a provider may not always need QCAT to enforce — but there are strict rules. Use Form R11 for rooming breaches.
  3. When in doubt, check the RTA's non-payment of rent page or call the RTA — timeframes are mandatory and easy to get wrong.

The homestay path (what actually applies to Kurrajong)

Because a genuine homestay is usually a boarder/lodger arrangement, you handle money and disputes through your agreement and your payment processor — not RTA bonds and tenancy notices.

If a student doesn't pay their board
  1. No lodged bond — a refundable hold. Kurrajong places a refundable pre-authorisation card hold at booking; it's only captured under the conditions in the agreement, and released otherwise. (Nothing goes to the RTA.)
  2. Rent flows host ↔ student via the processor (Stripe Connect), so money never sits with Kurrajong and each payment is tracked.
  3. Friendly reminder first. Most missed payments are admin slips — a same-day nudge usually resolves it.
  4. Kurrajong mediation. If it's not resolved, Kurrajong's dispute process steps in to agree a fix (catch-up plan, or an orderly end).
  5. End the placement per the agreement. The homestay agreement sets the notice period and what's owed — follow it rather than tenancy notices.
  6. Recovering a genuine debt. An unpaid amount is a civil debt; it can be pursued via QCAT's minor civil dispute process or small claims if needed. Keep records of every payment and message.

Which regime applies? (confirm with RTA / OFT / a lawyer)

  • Homestay / boarder-lodger — lives as part of the household, meals provided, host retains real control of the home → generally outside the Act (use your agreement + processor).
  • Rooming accommodation — a room with shared facilities, little "household" life, resident has exclusive use of the room → likely covered (RTA bond, R-series forms, faster arrears process).
  • General tenancy — a self-contained place with exclusive possession → covered (Form 18a, Form 2 bond, Form 11/12, QCAT).
